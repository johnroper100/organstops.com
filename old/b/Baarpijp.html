Audsley and Wedgwood list Baarpyp as a synonym for Baarpfeife, but do not mention Baarpijp. The name Gemshorn-Baarpijp appears only in Williams, who says only that it was a 17th-century stop. It is not clear whether Gemshorn-Baarpijp was an actual stop-name, or Williams was referring to the type of Baarpijp which resembles the Gemshorn.

Examples

Osiris contains 30 examples of Baarpijp, most of them at 8', with a handful at 4'. All but one are flues (see below). The earliest known examples of Baarpijp are cited below, along with the only known examples of Baarpyp, both of which are flues.

Baarpijp 8', Bovenwerk; St. John's, Schiedam, Holland; Neijenhoff c1600.

Baarpijp 8', Oberwerk; Nieuwe Kerk, Amsterdam, Holland; Schonat 1655.

Baarpijp 8', Bovenwerk; Laurenskerk, Alkmaar, The Netherlands; 1685.

Baarpijp 16', Borstwerk (prepared); Crystal Cathedral, Garden Grove, California, USA; Ruffatti. (This reed stop must be considered a misspelling of Baarpfeife.)

Baarpyp 8', Bovenmanuaal; Westerkerk, Amsterdam, Netherlands; Duyschot 1686.

Baarpyp 8', Hoofdwerk; Immanuelkerk, Ermelo, Netherlands; Heerde 1981.
